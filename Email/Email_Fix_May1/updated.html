A Message from Tristan






 

The COVID-19 pandemic has brought unprecedented changes to daily life. Sodexo has responded with a carefully coordinated effort that ensures our teams have the right tools and information to reduce risks to employees, students, and staff. Because the well-being of our employees, partners, and consumers is our highest priority, we’ve augmented our already stringent health and safety protocols and placed a renewed focus on training to ensure strict adherence to all guidelines.
 
During times of crisis, we look for opportunities to support one another and provide for the most vulnerable members of our community. The pandemic has created both personal and professional challenges for our employees, and our human resources teams have implemented support tools, such as modified sick time policies and counseling resources, to ease those burdens.
 
In addition, we strive to support our local communities. From food drives to volunteering for hunger-fighting efforts, our teams are more committed than ever to improving quality of daily life for students, staff, and community members.
 
As we continue to navigate this uncharted path together, I’m proud to share a few resources for maintaining physical and mental well-being and keeping students engaged. We look forward to collaborating with you regarding our return to school, and to implementing the new normal that will continue to keep our school communities safe and thriving.
Virtual Cooking Tutorials

Sodexo’s Kitchen Academy makes it possible to connect with students, parents, and teachers while sharing knowledge about cooking and food safety. Chefs and managers from 15 of our Independent Schools have live-streamed or posted videos of easy-to-follow culinary tutorials, with more in the works. Whether it be "Baking Cookies with Chef Sean-Michael" or "How to Cut an Onion with Chef Paul" these virtual classes offer a fun way to maintain student engagement and encourage ongoing participation.
Safeguarding Mental Health
Although remaining in our homes and drastically limiting social interaction are vital to controlling the spread of COVID-19, isolation has detrimental effects on mental health. Estrangement from family, friends, teachers, and colleagues amplifies feelings of anxiety and stress, which may even weaken immune systems. A new white publication, “Belonging from a Distance: Nurturing Social-Emotional Health During COVID-19,” explores options for helping campus communities stay connected and safeguarding mental health in the time of social distancing.
